Why it matters locally

COPDhub integrates with annual review workflows to give patients a personalised digital care plan they can use year-round. Its 8-year NHS Wales deployment demonstrates population-scale adoption across all deprivation levels — making it a strong exemplar for commissioners considering equitable digital access.

🌿 20,303 tonnes CO₂ equivalent reduction from inhaler prescribing shift in Wales (2018–2024). Relevant for ICB Net Zero commitments.

Clinical evidence

Full evidence record from the HTE19 dossier. Links to source publications provided where available.

Population-scale real-world evidence from 8-year NHS Wales national deployment (100% GP practice coverage). No standalone peer-reviewed RCT for COPDhub identified. Implementation science publication in Clinical Medicine (RCP) 2025 (Barry & Davies, PMC12395526). NICE HTE19 reviewed and recommended with conditions. ICST outcomes data shows 36% GP visit reduction and 19% A&E reduction among regular users.

Real-world, observational & service evaluation evidence (2)
Real-world evidence
NHS Wales national deployment (2019–present)
ICST / NHS Wales · 2019
ICST outcomes pageCase study ↗

Key results: 36% reduction in GP visits among regular users. 19% reduction in A&E admissions among regular users. 41% improvement in wellness scores at 4+ months use.

Limitation: ICST-reported outcomes. Not independently peer-reviewed. Regular users only — outcomes do not represent the full deployed population.

Real-world evidence
Low-GWP inhaler prescribing shift — NHS Wales (2018–2024)
ICST / NHS Wales · 2024
ICST outcomes page

Key results: Low-GWP inhaler prescribing shifted from 30.8% to 41.1% DDD (2018–2024). 20,303 tonnes CO₂ equivalent reduction. Most significant prescribing shift of any UK nation.

Limitation: Attributable to the ICST toolkit broadly — not to COPDhub alone. Reflects combined technology, clinical engagement and behaviour change work.

Population-scale outcomes from NHS Wales show 36% GP visit reduction and 19% A&E reduction among regular users. No formal cost-effectiveness model for COPDhub specifically — to be generated through NICE HTE19 evidence programme.

No formal cost-effectiveness model identified for COPDhub specifically. Commissioners should use local COPD prevalence, GP visit and A&E cost data to model potential impact based on published outcome signals.

Implementation
Model: App freely available on App Store (NHS Wales branded). NHS-branded referral pathway from GP. ICST implementation facilitators support rollout in each region.
Monitoring: Guided self-help model. Clinician messaging confirmed. Full clinician dashboard specification not publicly confirmed — clarify with ICST before deployment.
Hours caveat: COPDhub is a guided self-management tool, not a continuously monitored remote management service. Ensure local escalation pathways are clear.
