Why it matters locally

Luscii enables ICBs to build virtual COPD wards at scale without lengthy configuration delays — 150+ pre-built NHS pathways are available immediately. NWL ICS enrolled 6,000 COPD patients one year ahead of schedule. G-Cloud 14 listing means no separate OJEU tender is required.

Financial and commercial considerations

Pricing model
Not publicly disclosed. Available via G-Cloud 14 on NHS Digital Marketplace — no separate OJEU tender required. Contact Luscii via luscii.co.uk.
Procurement routes
Listed on NHS G-Cloud 14 (Digital Marketplace) as 'System-wide Remote Patient Monitoring Service' — procurement without separate OJEU tender is a significant process advantage.
NHSE £125k funding
Eligibility for NHSE ICB £125k COPD funding not confirmed for Luscii in reviewed sources. Verify with NHSE before including in business case.

NWL ICS enrolled 6,000 COPD patients ahead of schedule. Bradford ICB reached 6,000 patients with NHSE investment. Evidence on admission reduction pending peer-reviewed publication.
Tariff considerations
No live tariff calculator available. NWL ICS programme scale suggests potential for significant bed-day savings at population level, but formal peer-reviewed cost model for COPD not identified. Model using local COPD prevalence and hospital admission costs.
ROI notes
Sunderland: 50% respiratory admission reduction cited (attribution caveated — see data quality note). NWL: 6,000 patients enrolled 12 months early. Financial ROI model pending NICE HTE19 evidence generation.

Implementation
Model: Introductory patient call model (as per NWL ICS). Patient sets up app and Bluetooth oximeter with clinical support. 150+ pre-built pathways available immediately from Luscii Library. Custom pathways via Luscii Builder (Medical Developer certification required).
Monitoring: Active remote management model. Clinical Engine AI generates alerts for missed measurements, out-of-range readings and patient comments. Must be reviewed by clinical team or contracted hub.
Hours caveat: Virtual ward model requires staffed monitoring capability — either in-house or contracted (e.g. MyCare24 model). Confirm monitoring hours before deployment.
