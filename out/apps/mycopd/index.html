Why it matters locally

COPD drives high unplanned admissions and significant GP workload. myCOPD gives patients a structured self-management toolkit — including a 6-week home PR programme — without requiring face-to-face PR capacity. The Pro tier adds a clinician dashboard for remote monitoring at population scale.

Clinical evidence

Full evidence record from the HTE19 dossier. Links to source publications provided where available.

Two RCTs (RESCUE n=41, EARLY n=60), five real-world studies including NHS Grampian (n=64) and NHS Highland (n=113), and a NICE MTG68 health economics review. NICE MTG68 modelled £86,297 cost saving per CCG for AECOPD (74% probability) and £22,779 per CCG for PR (86% probability). Note: CCG-era model — recalculate using current ICB population sizes.

Randomised controlled trials (2)
RCTPeer-reviewed
RESCUE trial (MMH-R02)
North M et al. · npj Digital Medicine · 2020 · 3:145
DOI ↗PubMed ↗
DOI: 10.1038/s41746-020-00347-7PMID: 33145441n = 41Setting: Portsmouth Hospitals NHS Trust

Key results: CAT score 4.49 points lower in myCOPD arm (95% CI −8.41 to −0.58). Critical inhaler errors: 101→20 (myCOPD) vs 100→72 (usual care). Exacerbations: 18 vs 34. OR for readmission 0.383 (0.074–1.987).

Limitation: Feasibility study — signals clinical benefit but powered for feasibility not efficacy.

RCTPeer-reviewed⚠ Data quality flag
EARLY trial (MMH-R03)
Crooks MG et al. · ERJ Open Research · 2020 · 6(4):00460-2020
DOI ↗PubMed ↗Trial reg ↗
DOI: 10.1183/23120541.00460-2020PMID: 33263052PMC: 7682704Trial reg: NCT03620630n = 60Setting: Multi-site England

Key results: No significant difference in CAT score (−1.27, 95% CI −4.47–1.92, p=0.44). Inhaler error OR 0.30 (p=0.061) favouring myCOPD. PAM OR 1.65 favouring myCOPD.

Limitation: Primary outcome (CAT) not met. p=0.061 for inhaler errors does not reach p<0.05 threshold.

⚠ Commissioner note: Company describes inhaler error result (p=0.061) as statistically significant. This does not meet the conventional p<0.05 threshold. Do not cite as definitive evidence without noting this caveat.
Real-world, observational & service evaluation evidence (4)
Service evaluation
NHS Grampian service evaluation (MMH-E04)
Stonehaven Medical Group & Aberdeenshire HSCP · 2020
my mhealth evidence catalogue
n = 64Setting: Primary care, Aberdeenshire, 5 months (2019–2020)

Key results: Rescue inhaler use: 3.17→2.13/week. Inhaler technique: 48%→91% correct. Reduction in unscheduled GP visits and hospital admissions. Led to ICB-level commissioning decision.

Limitation: Service evaluation — not peer-reviewed. Reflects specific local deployment conditions.

ObservationalPeer-reviewed
NHS Highland feasibility study
Cooper R et al. · JMIR mHealth and uHealth · 2022 · 10(2):e30782
DOI ↗PubMed ↗
DOI: 10.2196/30782PMID: 35129453PMC: 8861861n = 113Setting: NHS Highland, up to 1 year

Key results: 78.8% activation rate. No correlation between app use and age, wealth, rurality or COPD severity. No significant population-level reduction in NHS resource use. Very high-use subgroup (17%) showed moderate reduction in admissions and bed days.

Limitation: No significant population-level reduction in NHS resource use in the full cohort.

Service evaluation
NHS Dorset population health study (MMH-E07)

my mhealth evidence catalogue (MMH-E07)

Key results: Integration with Dorset Intelligence and Insight Service (DiiS). Socioeconomic factors did not negatively influence activation. Clinician remote monitoring of CAT scores at population level demonstrated.

Limitation: Service evaluation — not peer-reviewed.

Financial and commercial considerations

Pricing model
Historically an unlimited population-based licence per CCG via 3-year contract. Current ICB-era pricing not publicly confirmed. Contact my mhealth for quote.
Procurement routes
Contract/licence agreement with my mhealth required before deployment. G-Cloud and direct ICB routes available.
NHSE £125k funding
Clinitouch is explicitly named in NHSE winter 2024 COPD funding. myCOPD eligibility not confirmed — verify with NHSE before including in business case.
Expected benefit
Potential to increase PR reach without additional face-to-face capacity. Evidence signals reduction in unplanned admissions and improvement in inhaler technique in high-engagement cohorts.
Tariff considerations
NICE MTG68 modelled £86,297 cost saving per CCG (AECOPD, 74% probability) and £22,779 per CCG (PR, 86% probability). These are CCG-era models — CCGs dissolved July 2022. Commissioners should recalculate using current ICB/PCN population size and local COPD prevalence. No live tariff calculator is available in this prototype.
ROI notes
NHS Grampian service evaluation showed reduction in unscheduled GP visits and admissions following deployment. Independent peer-reviewed cost data is limited.

Implementation
Model: Commissioner engagement → infrastructure assessment → pathway embedding with clinical teams → staff training → patient invitation or self-referral. Digital Health Advisors support patients.
Monitoring: Essentials: patient self-managed. Pro: clinician dashboard with remote monitoring — not a 24/7 monitored service. Patients must have clear escalation routes for deterioration.
Hours caveat: This is not a continuously monitored service. Ensure patients have access to appropriate clinical escalation routes.
